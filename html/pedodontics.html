Pediatric Dentistry

A Pediatric Dentist (also known as a Pedodontist); is a dentist specializing in child dental healthcare from infancy through to teenage years. Child oral healthcare is complex because kids are growing quickly, and their needs are constantly changing.

When it comes to the oral health of your child’s primary teeth (Milky Teeth), it is best to start practicing good oral hygiene as soon as the first baby tooth appears in the mouth. We ensure that your child’s teeth stay healthy and free from decay and other dental diseases.

Parent Consultation

A parent consultation session is done during the first visit to discuss the medical history of the child and also discuss the purpose of the visit.

Behavior management

We know how overwhelming a visit to the dentist can be for your little one. Our ’Behavior Management’ program helps your child bond with our dental healthcare provider by eliminating fear and anxiety while familiarizing the little patient with the tools and equipment.

What do pedodontists do?

Oral evaluation and assessment of your child’s teeth and gums.

Fluoride treatment.

Fissure sealants.

Treatment of decayed teeth.

Care for dental Injuries.

Pediatric dental emergencies.

Oral habit counseling.

Management and treatment of patients with special needs.

Management of developmental teeth disturbances.

Space maintenance after early tooth extraction.

Minor oral surgeries including: extractions, removal of extra teeth, and management of soft tissue pathology.

How often should I take my child to the dentist?

We recommend a check-up, every six months with a pediatric dentist.

What if my child finds it difficult to sit still in the dentist’s chair?

pedodontists and dental nurses are able to manage uncooperative young children and help shape their behavior toward a more positive outcome. Our team will engage the child in story telling about teeth and how to look after them, while the child is sitting in the dental chair. In most cases treatment is provided using gentle, local anesthesia. Alternatively, , our expert team uses safe methods such as inhalation sedation (nitrous oxide or happy gas) or if required, general anesthesia.
